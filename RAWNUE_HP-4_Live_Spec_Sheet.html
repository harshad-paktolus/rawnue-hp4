Protein margin check

Yeast has nucleic-acid nitrogen — crude protein (N×6.25) can overstate true protein. Ask the lab for method + N-factor.

04

R&D mixing protocol

  1. Master-batch first. ≥10 servings, tumble/sieve-blend 2–3× so micro-ingredients distribute. You can't dose 0.05 g Reb M per cup.
  2. Portion 40 g per test cup.
  3. Two bases in parallel: 40 g into 200–250 ml cold milk, and 40 g into cold water.
  4. Shake 20–30 s with a mixing ball or frother.
  5. Rest 1–2 min to hydrate, then re-shake. Cuts the powdery perception.
  6. Serve 5–8 °C over ice. Test 200 ml vs 250 ml.
  7. Blind panel of 3–5. Benchmark HP-4 vs HP-2 (flavour) and HP-3 (texture).

    NO
    "Complete protein"/PDCAAS without amino-acid data. No digestion, gut, energy, hormone, immunity, muscle, "clean"/"detox" claims.
    09

    Regulatory & allergen flags

    Monk fruit — verify approval. Reb M/stevia are FSSAI-permitted; monk fruit/mogrosides do not have the same clear approval in India. Keep stevia (raw #12) as fallback.
    Product category unresolved. Nutraceutical vs Proprietary Food changes ingredients, claims, label format. Settle with a regulatory consultant first.
    Allergen cross-contact. Declare "may contain soy, milk". Oats → gluten cross-contact; no GF claim without certified GF oats + testing.
    Cocoa heavy metals. Cadmium/lead — meet FSSAI limits; test.
    Labelling (2020). Per serving & per 100 g, added-sugars line, ingredients descending, green mark, FSSAI logo + licence no.
    Brand name. "Energy" must read as brand, not a functional claim.
    10

    Final recommendation

    Test first: lead with HP-4; run HP-2 (flavour) and HP-3 (texture) in the same blind panel.

    Lab (finished blend): proximate + protein method/N-factor, total & added sugars, heavy metals (Cd/Pb), full micro, water activity, stability study. Push yeast supplier for protein % + amino-acid profile.

    Hold every claim until the finished-blend report is in hand. Add "may contain soy, milk" now.
